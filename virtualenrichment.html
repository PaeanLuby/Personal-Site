Virtual Enrichment

Description

Enriched environments, or sensory stimulating environments, have been around since the 1960s in rodents, and have been shown to be highly conducive to promoting neuroplasticity following a neural insult. However, in clinical settings, these environments are difficult to standardize and implement because of their cost and complexity. Virtual reality could help address both of these disadvantages, allowing for enriched environment protocols to be reproducible across patients and accessible to the general public. For this reason, this summer, I designed and implemented a preclinical study comparing virtual and physical versions of enriched environments with the same novel stimuli. My investigation’s behavioral data suggests that the virtual environment changed the rats’ openness to explore in the same way as the physical environment. This semester, I am analyzing the vasculature and neural circuitry of these rats’ visual cortices, somatosensory cortices, and hippocampi to infer possible neuroscientific bases for these similarities, along with using machine learning to further quantify rodent behavior.

Implementation

I used a combination of projectors, image, and video-editing software to construct the virtual environment.

Using Machine Learning for Human-Following Robots

Description

Human-robot interaction is the study of relationships between humans and robots. Its applications range from facilitating socialization and rehabilitation to providing caregiving support, and frequently involve person-following scenarios, where humans and robots collaborate on tasks involving the robotâ€™s proximity to a designated person. However, many person-following algorithms depend on computationally expensive algorithms, making them impractical for computationally constrained unmanned ground vehicles. This project sought to compare three object detection algorithms (YOLOv5nano, small, and medium) in their completion of a human-following task.

Implementation

This program was built with Python, Gazebo, and ROS 2.
